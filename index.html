FIRMWARE

CALLUM CLARKE

My personal values are strongly influenced by my faith as a Christian. With that foundation, I've identified five traits that I strive to demonstrate through my actions in both my professional and personal life.

Wisdom
  • Actively seeking to understand the viewpoints and opinions of others to better inform my own views
  • Preferring to listen rather than speak, particularly when the topic of discussion is new to me
  • Ensuring that I gather and weigh pros and cons before making decisions that have a lasting impact for myself and others
Innovation
  • Trying, failing, learning, and trying again
  • Investing time into a variety of projects, and prioritising those that show signs of success
  • Not being afraid of being/doing different, but also not being/doing different for the sake of it
Curiosity
  • Taking an interest in things that are new to me, and making sure that I try things that scare me every once in a while
  • Seeking to broaden my knowledge on topics that I find interesting, be that History, Geography or something altogether more abstract!
  • Making time for both small and large adventures
Integrity
  • Making sure that at all times, I do "the right thing", particularly inter-personally
  • Being honest even when it comes at a personal cost
  • Having ownership of solving or delivering things; following things through until everyone agrees that they're complete
Humility
  • Having strong opinions that are only weakly held, and therefore being willing to change my mind when presented with better evidence
  • Avoiding boastfulness or public self-congratulation
  • Recognising when others should be elevated and helping to make sure that happens

Ultimately, achieving each of these traits is about being able to assist others in finding and experiencing Joy in their lives. For me, Joy can best be described as a feeling of deep satisfaction, appreciation and wholeness. Joy can be found via the broad brushstrokes of our lives but also in the smaller details. Lastly, I'd like to share my take on what I think makes a great team. These are the building blocks that every great team is built with:

Empowered specialists
Shared short-term and long-term goals
Trust
Positive outlooks
Concise and effective communication
Clear division of tasks
Personal accountability
Leadership by example
Commitment to collaboration
Diversity being celebrated
Respect

You can spot which teams have been able to embrace each of these concepts via the results they achieve:

  • Deadlines and sprint goals are nearly always met. When they aren't, everyone understands why and agrees what to do about it.
  • Information doesn't feel silo'd. All team members actively engage in avoiding this, both via knowledge-sharing and up-skilling.
  • The team strikes a balance between quality and speed of delivery. Trust is a two-way street between QA and PM, with risks jointly evaluated.
  • Most issues are found and rectified early on in the software development life cycle.
  • Quality processes are ever-evolving. There is a clear process for retrospective- and improvement- in light of particularly major bugs.
  • Innovation can come from anyone in the team, even the more junior members.
  • Scrum ceremonies don't feel laborious. Instead they are focused encounters that the team feels is enabling them to do their best work.
  • Project and product management retains a clear view of progress, with no last minute surprises.
  • Developers don't feel overworked, nor that they are spending too much time in meetings. They are proud of the code they produce.
  • Team-wide responsibilities, such as releases, supporting the wider business and package/security updates are shared equally.
